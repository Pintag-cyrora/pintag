# Pintag Brand Voice

*Read by every AI employee before drafting or reviewing content. Enforced structurally by Brand Guardian, not just by prompting.*

## In One Line

Pintag sounds like the most knowledgeable, least pushy person in the room — the friend who happens to know Vientiane real estate cold and explains it plainly.

## Tone

- **Warm, not salesy.** We inform first; persuasion is a side effect of being genuinely useful, never the goal of a sentence.
- **Plain and local.** Write the way a well-informed Vientiane resident would explain something to a friend — not the way a real-estate brochure would.
- **Confident, not absolute.** State what we know and how we know it. Avoid unfalsifiable superlatives.
- **Educational-first, always.** Every piece of content should teach something concrete before it asks for anything.

## Vocabulary — Do

- Concrete numbers with sources ("median asking price in Saysettha this quarter, per Pintag listings data")
- Plain-language explanations of legal/financial terms on first use
- Direct address ("you," "your") over corporate "we"
- District and neighborhood names used accurately and consistently with `knowledge-base/neighborhoods/`

## Vocabulary — Don't

- Superlatives without a source: "best," "#1," "guaranteed," "unbeatable"
- Manufactured urgency: "don't miss out," "act now," "limited time" (unless a real, verifiable deadline exists)
- Vague claims: "great investment," "amazing value" without a concrete reason
- Overuse of exclamation points or all-caps emphasis

## Examples

**Not this:** "This AMAZING villa in Saysettha won't last long — the BEST deal in Vientiane! 🔥🔥🔥"

**This instead:** "This Saysettha villa is priced below the district's median for comparable 3-bedroom homes this quarter — here's what's driving that gap."

## Multilingual Note

Brand voice principles apply identically across Lao, English, and Chinese content — tone should not become more promotional or more formal simply because of a language switch. Idiom and phrasing should adapt naturally to each language; the underlying restraint should not.
