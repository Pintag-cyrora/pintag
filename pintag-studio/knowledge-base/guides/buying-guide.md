# Buying Guide: Purchasing Property in Vientiane

A practical step-by-step overview for buyers navigating a property purchase on Pintag.

## Step 1: Define Budget and District Priorities

Decide on a firm budget range and shortlist two or three districts based on commute, schools, and lifestyle. See `knowledge-base/neighborhoods/` for district-by-district profiles.

## Step 2: Browse and Shortlist Listings

Use Pintag to filter by property type (house, villa, apartment, townhouse, land, or commercial), district, and price range, and build a shortlist of properties worth viewing in person.

## Step 3: Arrange Viewings

Contact the listing agent or owner directly through the listing's contact information to arrange an in-person viewing. Bring a list of questions about the property's history, documentation, and any needed repairs.

## Step 4: Verify Documentation

Before making an offer, verify the seller's land title or right-to-use documentation, confirm there are no outstanding disputes or liens, and check that physical boundaries match the documentation. Buyers should engage a local lawyer or notary for this step. [to be refined by founder]

## Step 5: Negotiate and Agree Terms

Negotiation on price and terms is standard practice in the Vientiane market. Agree on price, payment schedule, and any conditions in writing before proceeding.

## Step 6: Complete the Transfer

Finalize payment and complete the official transfer of title or right-to-use documentation through the appropriate local authority, typically with legal counsel involved to ensure the transfer is properly registered. [to be refined by founder]

## Step 7: Post-Purchase Registration

Update utility accounts, local registration, and any applicable tax records to reflect the new ownership.

## Note for Foreign Buyers

Foreign buyers face restrictions on direct land ownership in Laos. See `knowledge-base/guides/foreign-ownership-rules.md` before proceeding with any purchase involving a non-Lao national buyer.
