# Selling Guide: Listing and Selling Property in Vientiane

A practical step-by-step overview for property owners selling on Pintag.

## Step 1: Prepare the Property

Address obvious repairs, declutter, and consider professional photography — listings with clear, well-lit photos consistently attract more inquiries.

## Step 2: Gather Documentation

Assemble land title or right-to-use documents, any prior sale agreements, and utility records. Having documentation ready in advance speeds up the transaction once a buyer is found.

## Step 3: Set a Realistic Price

Compare against similar recent listings in the same district and property type. Overpricing is the most common reason listings sit unsold for months. See `knowledge-base/market/laos-real-estate-overview.md` for general pricing context.

## Step 4: Create the Listing

Submit the property to Pintag with complete details: property type, district, size, price, photos, and a clear description. Complete listings are prioritized in search results and Pintag's own content.

## Step 5: Respond to Inquiries Promptly

Buyers and their agents often compare multiple properties simultaneously; slow responses lose deals. Aim to respond to inquiries within a day.

## Step 6: Negotiate

Be prepared for negotiation on price and terms — this is standard practice in the Vientiane market rather than an exception.

## Working with Agents

Sellers who list through an agent-partner benefit from local market knowledge and an existing buyer network; Pintag supports both owner-direct and agent-assisted listings.
