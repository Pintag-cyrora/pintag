# Renting Guide: Renting Property in Vientiane

A practical step-by-step overview for renters using Pintag.

## Step 1: Define Requirements

Decide on budget, preferred district, furnishing needs, and lease length. See `knowledge-base/neighborhoods/` for district profiles to help narrow the search.

## Step 2: Search and Shortlist

Filter Pintag listings by property type, district, and monthly rent to build a shortlist of candidates.

## Step 3: Arrange Viewings

Contact the listing agent or owner to schedule viewings. Check water pressure, electrical systems, and internet availability during the visit, as these vary significantly between properties.

## Step 4: Clarify Lease Terms

Before committing, confirm the lease length, deposit amount, what is included in rent (utilities, internet, maintenance), and the process for renewing or ending the lease early.

## Step 5: Deposit and Move-In

Standard practice is one to three months' deposit plus the first month's rent upfront. Get a signed lease agreement in the language you are most comfortable with, ideally with an English or Lao translation available for reference. [to be refined by founder]

## Step 6: Document Property Condition

Photograph the property's condition at move-in to avoid disputes over the deposit at move-out.

## Step 7: Renewing or Ending the Lease

Notify the landlord within the timeframe specified in the lease if planning to renew or vacate, typically 30 to 60 days in advance.

## Note for Expatriate Renters

Expatriate renters should confirm whether a lease agreement needs to be registered with local authorities as part of visa or work-permit requirements. [to be refined by founder]
