# About Pintag

## What Pintag Is

Pintag is a real-estate marketplace built for Vientiane, Laos, connecting property buyers, renters, and investors with verified listings and local agents. Founded by Keomany, Pintag was created to bring transparency and structure to a property market that has historically relied on word-of-mouth, informal Facebook groups, and fragmented agency listings scattered across many small websites.

The platform lists houses, villas, apartments, townhouses, land, and commercial property across all districts of Vientiane, with each listing showing price, location, size, and photos in a consistent, comparable format.

## Mission

Pintag's mission is to make finding, buying, selling, and renting property in Vientiane as straightforward as it is in more mature real-estate markets — without losing the local knowledge and relationships that make Lao real estate work. Concretely, that means: verified listings buyers can trust, clear pricing information, and a direct line to agents and property owners who know the neighborhoods they operate in.

## Trilingual Positioning

Vientiane's property market serves three overlapping audiences: local Lao buyers and renters, English-speaking expatriates and regional investors, and a growing Chinese-speaking buyer and investor segment tied to regional trade and infrastructure investment. Pintag is built trilingual from the ground up — Lao, English, and Chinese — rather than treating any one language as primary and the others as translations bolted on afterward. This matters for marketing as much as for the product: content, listings, and communication should feel native in each language, not translated.

## Why This Matters for Marketing

Every piece of content Pintag's marketing produces should reinforce two things: that Pintag is a trustworthy, well-informed local resource on Vientiane real estate, and that it serves all three language communities equally well. Educational, substance-first content is the foundation of that trust — promotion follows credibility, not the other way around.
