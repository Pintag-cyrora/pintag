# Audience Personas

Pintag's marketing content should be written with one of these four personas in mind. Most pieces primarily serve one persona but should not actively confuse or mislead the others.

## 1. The Buyer

A local Lao professional or family, or an expatriate resident, looking to purchase a home to live in rather than to invest. Typically has a defined budget and a shortlist of preferred districts based on commute, schools, or family ties. Cares most about price transparency, legitimate ownership documentation, and understanding the real difference between neighborhoods. Often a first-time buyer who does not know the buying process end-to-end and is wary of being overcharged or misled.

## 2. The Renter

Often a younger professional, an expatriate on a work assignment, or a student, looking for a house, apartment, or townhouse to rent, usually on a 6-to-12-month horizon. Cares about move-in speed, clear lease terms, proximity to work or international schools, and furnishing status. Less price-sensitive than the Buyer persona in absolute terms but very sensitive to hidden fees and deposit practices.

## 3. The Investor

A regional or international buyer, frequently Chinese-speaking, evaluating Vientiane property for rental yield or long-term appreciation rather than personal use. Cares about foreign ownership rules, leasehold structures, rental demand by district, and comparison to other regional markets. Needs clear, honest information about legal constraints on foreign ownership before anything else — this persona is quick to disengage if content feels like it is glossing over legal complexity.

## 4. The Agent-Partner

A Vientiane-based real-estate agent or small agency that lists properties on Pintag on behalf of clients. Cares about lead quality, how quickly Pintag content and listings generate inquiries, and being seen as credible by association with the Pintag brand. This persona is also a distribution channel — content that agent-partners are proud to share extends Pintag's reach organically.
