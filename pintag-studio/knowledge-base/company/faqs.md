# Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions from Pintag buyers and renters, with short reference answers for content use.

**1. Can foreigners buy property in Laos?**
Foreign ownership of land is restricted under Lao law. Foreign buyers typically use long-term leasehold arrangements or condominium-unit ownership structures instead of direct land title. See `guides/foreign-ownership-rules.md` for details — this is a legally sensitive area and should be verified before publishing anything specific.

**2. What documents should I check before buying a property?**
Buyers should confirm the seller's land title (or equivalent right-to-use document), check for any outstanding disputes or liens, and verify boundaries match what is being sold. [to be refined by founder]

**3. How is rent typically paid in Vientiane?**
Rent is commonly quoted and paid monthly, with landlords often requesting one to three months' deposit plus the first month's rent upfront. Longer leases sometimes negotiate reduced deposits.

**4. Are prices on Pintag listed in Lao Kip or US Dollars?**
Property prices in Vientiane are frequently quoted in US Dollars for larger transactions, with rent sometimes quoted in Kip for local tenants. Pintag listings aim to show both where possible. [to be refined by founder]

**5. How long does a typical property sale take in Vientiane?**
Timelines vary widely by property type and documentation readiness, but buyers should expect the process to take longer than in markets with fully digitized land registries.

**6. What is the difference between a villa and a townhouse listing?**
A villa is typically a detached single-family home with its own land plot, while a townhouse is an attached or semi-detached unit sharing walls with neighboring units, usually in a planned row.

**7. Can I negotiate the price on a listing?**
Yes, negotiation is common practice in the Vientiane market, particularly for direct sales rather than new development units.

**8. Does Pintag verify every listing?**
Pintag reviews listings submitted by agents and property owners for completeness and plausibility, though buyers should still perform their own due diligence before any transaction. [to be refined by founder]

**9. What areas of Vientiane does Pintag cover?**
Pintag covers all seven core districts of Vientiane: Sisattanak, Saysettha, Chanthabouly, Sikhottabong, Xaythany, Hadxaifong, and Naxaithong.

**10. How do I contact an agent about a listing?**
Each listing on Pintag includes direct contact information for the listing agent or owner so buyers and renters can reach out without an intermediary step.
