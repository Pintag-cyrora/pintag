Pintag's non-negotiable content principle (`brain/ceo.md` → Non-Negotiable Principles) is that educational value always outranks promotional language: "If a piece of content teaches nothing, it doesn't ship, no matter how well it might perform." This is enforced structurally, not just by prompting — it's the highest-weighted dimension in the Content Quality Score (`brain/org-config.json` → `quality_score.weights.educational_value: 2.0`, the only dimension weighted above 1.5).

**The hook pattern this produces:** lead with a concrete, specific, verifiable fact or explanation — never with urgency or superlatives. Compare the two examples already codified in `brain/brand-voice.md`:

- Avoid: "This AMAZING villa in Saysettha won't last long — the BEST deal in Vientiane!"
- Prefer: "This Saysettha villa is priced below the district's median for comparable 3-bedroom homes this quarter — here's what's driving that gap."

The second version *is* the hook — a reader stops scrolling because they learn something concrete, not because they're told to hurry. Persuasion is a side effect of being genuinely useful, never the stated goal of a sentence.
