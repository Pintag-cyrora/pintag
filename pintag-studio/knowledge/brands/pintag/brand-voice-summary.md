Pintag "sounds like the most knowledgeable, least pushy person in the room — the friend who happens to know Vientiane real estate cold and explains it plainly" (`brain/brand-voice.md`).

**Hard constraints (never violate, any language, any content type):**
- No unsourced superlatives: "best," "#1," "guaranteed," "unbeatable."
- No manufactured urgency: "don't miss out," "act now," "limited time" — unless a real, verifiable deadline exists.
- No vague claims ("great investment," "amazing value") without a concrete, sourced reason attached.
- Concrete numbers always carry a source, e.g. "median asking price in Saysettha this quarter, per Pintag listings data."

**Multilingual note:** these constraints apply identically across Lao, English, and Chinese — tone should not become more promotional or more formal just because the language switches. Idiom and phrasing adapt naturally per language; the underlying restraint does not (`brain/brand-voice.md` → Multilingual Note).
