Vientiane's real estate market is smaller and less formalized than neighboring capitals (Bangkok, Hanoi): historically organized through personal networks, informal Facebook marketplace groups, and small local agencies rather than centralized listing platforms. There's no single centralized public price registry, so pricing is inconsistent across sources and comparable-sale data is harder to access than in more mature markets.

**Distinctive pattern worth remembering for content:** a meaningful share of listed properties are advertised as available for either sale *or* rent, with the owner's preference shifting with market conditions (liquidity need vs. steady income). Treat "for sale" / "for rent" as a point-in-time attribute of a listing, not a permanent classification — owners switch between the two.

**Typical property types:** House, Villa (concentrated in/around Sisattanak and parts of Saysettha), Apartment (central districts, popular with renters/younger professionals), Townhouse (mid-market, newer developments), Land (often bought for future development or long-term holding, given foreign-ownership restrictions favor land-based holding by Lao nationals — see `knowledge-base/guides/foreign-ownership-rules.md` before writing anything about this), Commercial.
