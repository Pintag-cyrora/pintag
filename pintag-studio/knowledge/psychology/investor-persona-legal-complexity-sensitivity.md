Of Pintag's four audience personas, the Investor (regional/international, frequently Chinese-speaking, evaluating Vientiane property for yield or appreciation rather than personal use) has the sharpest trust trigger: this persona "is quick to disengage if content feels like it is glossing over legal complexity" around foreign ownership rules, leasehold structures, and rental demand (`knowledge-base/company/audience-personas.md`).

**Practical implication for Writer/Brand Guardian:** content aimed at this persona should surface legal constraints plainly and early, not as a caveat buried after the pitch — the opposite of typical marketing instinct (lead with upside, footnote the complexity). This is also where the strictest editorial gate in the whole system applies: any new foreign land-ownership claim requires explicit founder/counsel sign-off before publication, regardless of Approval Phase (`knowledge-base/guides/foreign-ownership-rules.md`, `brain/ceo.md` Non-Negotiable Principles) — so Investor-persona content is disproportionately likely to need that sign-off, and should be planned with that lead time in mind rather than treated as routine educational output.
